\## 1. Our Brand

\### 1.1 Our Story

Co/Unity was born from the vision to create a space where community and
unity converge. We believe in the power of bringing people together
online and offline, through shared experiences, collaborative efforts,
and mutual support.

We started with a simple observation: Most brands don’t have a growth problem; they have a retention problem. In an "attention economy" that treats people like clicks, the true value of human connection was being lost in the noise.

Our approach is rooted in an analytical and engineering mindset. We don’t just "build groups"; we design scalable infrastructure. We look at community through a data-oriented lens—mapping value-exchange systems and engineering the conduits that allow energy to flow between a brand and its members. We believe that if you can’t measure the "Proof of Advocacy," you can’t scale it.

But infrastructure without soul is just code. At our core, we recognize that the desire for identity, belonging, and common unity is hardwired into the human makeup. We use technology and data as the "how," but human connection is the "why." By blending the precision of engineering with the deep alchemy of belonging, we help brands move beyond vanity metrics to create ecosystems where people truly stick.

\-\--

\### 1.8 Brand Name

\*\*Co/Unity\*\*

The name was born at the intersection of two powerful ideas:
\*\*COMMON\*\* and \*\*UNITY\*\*.

\- \*\*Common\*\* represents shared values, goals, and vision. -
\*\*Unity\*\* represents the strength that comes from coming together.

The evolution explored the family of \"co-\" words --- collaborate,
connect, coalesce, converge, collective --- all speaking to the essence
of bringing people, ideas, and possibilities together.

\*\*Name Evolution:\*\* \`\`\` C O M M O N U N I T Y → C O M M U N I T Y
→ CO/UNITY \`\`\`

\- \*\*Co/\*\* stands for collaboration, co-creation, and community. The
slash is the bridge --- linking people and ideas, moving us forward. -
\*\*Unity\*\* is the destination: a place where collaboration transforms
into collective strength.

\-\--

\### 1.9 Brand Name --- Typography Usage Guidelines

\*\*Headers & Subheaders: ALL CAPS → \`CO/UNITY\`\*\*

Use CO/UNITY in all caps in prominent placements such as headers,
subheaders, and key visual statements. This reinforces the brand\'s
bold, confident, and unified identity.

\*\*Body Text: Title Case → \`Co/Unity\`\*\*

In body copy, use Co/Unity in title case to maintain readability and
flow in longer paragraphs. Title case integrates more naturally with
sentence structures and ensures the brand name feels approachable,
conversational, and human.

\*\*Why this matters:\*\*

\- \*\*Visual hierarchy\*\* -- All caps in headers create a strong,
attention-grabbing focal point; title case in body text supports
comfortable reading. - \*\*Consistency of voice\*\* -- The shift in
casing between display and body contexts subtly balances the dual
personality: bold and authoritative when leading, warm and human when
speaking. - \*\*Brand recognition\*\* -- Co/Unity\'s distinctive slash
remains constant in all contexts, anchoring the visual identity while
the casing adapts for function and tone.

\## 2. Logo

\### 2.1 Wordmark Logo

The logo is the most recognizable and iconic way to signal the Co/Unity
brand. It embodies the essence of connection and community and should be
used consistently across all platforms to maintain brand recognition.

\*\*Logo Asset Downloads:\*\* Logo pack available in PNG & SVG formats.

\-\--

\### 2.2 Logo --- Background Usage

\*\*On Colours:\*\* When overlaying the signature on brand colors, brand
artwork, or photography, always ensure there is enough contrast to meet
accessibility standards.

\- On \*\*dark backgrounds\*\* (Tahitian Tide, Black): Use \*\*white\*\*
logo - On \*\*light backgrounds\*\* (White, Mint): Use \*\*dark\*\*
logo - On \*\*Pink Dolphin\*\*: Use \*\*white\*\* logo - On
\*\*Black\*\*: Use \*\*white\*\* logo

\*\*On Photography & Artwork:\*\* Ensure enough contrast to meet
accessibility standards. Avoid placing the logo on busy or low-contrast
imagery.

\-\--

\### 2.3 Logo --- Clear Space

The exclusion zone is equal to the \*\*slash \`/\`\*\* in the logo
wordmark. This zone should be considered the absolute minimum safe
distance. In most cases, give the logo even more room.

\-\--

\### 2.4 Logo --- Minimum Size

\*\*24px\*\* minimum height for adequate legibility in digital and print
applications.

\-\--

\### 2.5 Logo --- Placement

Place the wordmark in consistent locations. This ensures simple,
consistent placement while allowing enough flexibility to accommodate
the dynamic graphic system.

\-\--

\### 2.6 Logo --- Misuse (DON\'T)

\- Don\'t squash or stretch - Don\'t add glows - Don\'t distort - Don\'t
crop - Don\'t add outlines - Don\'t alter logotype - Don\'t use any
off-brand colours or gradients

\-\--

\### 2.7 Monograms

Two monogram options: 1. \*\*CO/\*\* --- Primary monogram extracted from
the wordmark 2. \*\*/\*\* --- Slash only, for special scenarios

Monograms can be used when the brand name is already represented or when
wordmark legibility is compromised.

\*\*Approved Use Cases:\*\* - Favicon / Browser tab icon - App icons or
mobile nav bar - Social media profile avatars - Event badges or
wristbands - Internal community tools - Stickers, pins, swag - Sub-brand
marks (e.g., CO/LABS)

\*\*Monogram Clear Space:\*\* Same rules as wordmark --- exclusion zone
equal to the slash \`/\`.

\-\--

\### 2.8 Partnership & Sponsor Lockups

When partnering with another brand, use the same clear space principles.
Achieving a balanced composition between the Co/Unity logo and partner
logo is crucial. Individual consideration may be necessary for each
lockup. Partner logos should be optically adjusted to create a more
equal hierarchy.

\## 5. Conceptual Forms

\### 5.1 Overview

Conceptual forms are abstract visual elements that symbolize the
brand\'s core ideas, philosophy, and mission. They go beyond decoration
--- they are symbolic design expressions rooted in concepts, metaphors,
or functions.

\-\--

\### 5.2 DNA Helix

The liquid, ribbon-like helix form reflects Co/Unity\'s identity as a
vital, adaptive part of the communities it serves.

\*\*Symbolism:\*\* The flowing, intertwined structure evokes a DNA
helix, symbolizing how Co/Unity embeds within communities. Like a
missing chromosome, we complete and enhance their structure.

\- We don\'t impose. We integrate. - We don\'t build beside you. We
build as you.

\*\*Liquid Glass Properties:\*\* - \*\*Adapts\*\* --- like tools that
flex to community needs - \*\*Reflects\*\* --- listening, adapting, and
mirroring values - \*\*Illuminates\*\* --- creativity that cuts through
noise - \*\*Communicates\*\* --- movement, progress, and unity

\*\*Keywords:\*\* Integration / Co-creation / Evolution /
Interconnection / Adaptability / Flow / Transformation

\-\--

\### 5.3 The Slash \`/\` --- Symbolism

In Web3, the \`/\` is the connective tissue --- bridging concepts,
signaling code culture, and inviting participation.

\*\*Duality & Intersection:\*\* The slash represents two concepts in
dialogue --- partnership, community, or the meeting point between ideas.
It suggests \"this and that,\" \"where X meets Y,\" or \"the bridge
between worlds.\"

\*\*Modular Identity in Code Culture:\*\* The \`/\` is native to URLs,
file paths, and command-line syntax --- speaking the language of
builders, devs, and crypto-native users.

\*\*Action & Navigation:\*\* In Discord, Notion, and many dApps, typing
\`/\` opens commands --- a shorthand for \"do something now.\" It aligns
with Web3\'s culture of agency and participation.

\*\*Decentralized Co-creation:\*\* The slash hints at permissionless
collaboration: side-by-side --- peers in a network, not hierarchy.

\-\--
